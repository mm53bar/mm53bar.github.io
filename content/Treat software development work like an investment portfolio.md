Balance short-term "bets" (quick wins, maintenance) and long-term "bets" (innovation, foundational work) to optimize outcomes and manage risk.

#### **Key Principles**

1. **Diversify work types**:
    
    - **Short-term bets** (<6 months):
        
        - _Examples_: Bug fixes, minor features, urgent client requests.
            
        - _Risk profile_: Low uncertainty, quick returns (e.g., user satisfaction, revenue).
            
        - _Overweight risk_: Stagnation, tech debt accumulation.
            
    - **Long-term bets** (6+ months):
        
        - _Examples_: R&D, architectural overhaul, new product lines.
            
        - _Risk profile_: High uncertainty, exponential returns (e.g., market leadership).
            
        - _Overweight risk_: Wasted resources if bets fail.
            
2. **Mix determines organizational risk**:
    
    - **Conservative portfolio** (e.g., 80% short-term, 20% long-term):
        
        - Minimizes short-term volatility but risks obsolescence.
            
    - **Aggressive portfolio** (e.g., 50% long-term):
        
        - Maximizes breakthrough potential but risks near-term instability.
            
3. **Dynamic rebalancing**:
    
    - Adjust the mix quarterly based on:
        
        - Market shifts (e.g., new competitors).
            
        - Team capacity (e.g., burnout from excessive short-term work).
            
        - Bet outcomes (e.g., kill underperforming long-term projects early).
            

#### **Why This Works**

- **Mitigates "correlation risk"**: Short-term work funds long-term bets; long-term bets prevent irrelevance.
    
- **Forces explicit trade-offs**: Prevents over-indexing on immediate demands _or_ speculative moonshots.
    
- **Aligns with human incentives**: Short-term wins sustain morale; long-term projects attract top talent.
    

#### **Common Pitfalls**

- **Portfolio imbalance**:
    
    - _All short-term_ → "Feature factory" mode (high busyness, low strategic impact).
        
    - _All long-term_ → "Research lab" mode (high cost, delayed value).
        
- **Misjudging bets**: Treating low-impact work as "strategic" (e.g., over-engineering).
    

#### **Actionable Insight**

**Audit your team’s portfolio quarterly**:

- Categorize all work into short-term vs. long-term.
    
- Calculate the ratio (e.g., 70/30) and ask: _"Does this match our risk tolerance?"_
    
- Shift resources to rebalance (e.g., scale long-term bets if short-term dominates).
    

---

**Atomic Summary**:

> Software teams should manage work like a financial portfolio—diversifying short-term "low-risk" tasks and long-term "high-risk" projects—to balance immediate stability against future growth. The ideal mix depends on organizational risk appetite and requires active rebalancing.
