Most teams treat _development speed_ as their bottleneck, but the real constraint is **learning speed**:

1. **Uncertainty dominates software work**. Dan North argues we should "measure and manage ignorance" like we do scope or budget. Example: A team building a fraud detector spends 80% of their time _understanding_ fraud patterns, not coding.

2. **Stable teams compound learning**:
    - Fowler’s product teams retain hard-won knowledge (e.g., why the fraud rules evolved), while project teams relearn from scratch.
    - Institutional knowledge lets them ask better questions faster ([[Deliberate discovery requires deep context]]).

3. **Implication**: Optimize for _learning continuity_. Example:   
    - A "fraud team" that stays together for years develops intuition for edge cases, while a project team ships a brittle solution.

**Connections**:

- Contrast with [[Outputs over outcomes]]: Project teams prioritize "done" over learning.
- Supports [[Stable teams gain more with AI]]: AI accelerates execution, but only teams with context can direct it wisely.
- Explains [[Look for leverage]]: learning continuity is what makes leverage sustainable — without persistent context, you can't find where the system actually bends.
