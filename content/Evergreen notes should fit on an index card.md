Each evergreen note should fit on a single index card—roughly 3-4 paragraphs maximum. This constraint forces clarity and prevents notes from becoming mini blog articles.

In the original Zettelkasten system, notes were literally written on index cards stored in drawers. The physical constraint meant you had to distill ideas to their essence. Digital notes remove this constraint, which often leads to notes that are too long to be useful building blocks.

When a note grows beyond index-card size, it's a signal to break it into multiple atomic notes. A long note about "soft deletion strategies" covering problems, solutions, and implementation becomes three cards: one on the problem, one on the architectural insight, one on the implementation technique.

Index-card-sized notes are easier to:
- Link to precisely (you know exactly what's on the other end of the link)
- Recombine in new contexts
- Maintain over time (small surface area for updates)
- Scan quickly when following links
