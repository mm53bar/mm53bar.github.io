Strategic opportunism is the practice of **pursuing short-term opportunities in service of long-term goals**. It’s not ad-hoc reactivity—it’s _prepared creativity_:

1. **Long-term vision as a scaffold**: Teams with clarity on objectives (e.g., "reduce system fragility") can recognize and act on small wins (e.g., refactoring a module during a bugfix).
2. **Tacit knowledge fuels opportunism**: Deep system understanding lets engineers spot "lucky" openings ([[Developers spend most of their time figuring out systems]]).
3. **AI amplifies opportunism**: By automating toil ([[Use AI to reduce toil]]), AI frees up mental bandwidth to _notice and exploit_ these opportunities—like tech debt paydowns during feature work.

**Implications:**

- Stable teams are better at this because they retain the context needed to _connect_ short-term actions to long-term strategy. ([[Stable teams gain more with AI]])
- Strategic opportunism is how you "make your own luck"—preparation meets serendipity.
