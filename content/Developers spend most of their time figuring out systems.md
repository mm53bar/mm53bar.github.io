A developer’s productivity bottleneck is rarely typing speed—it’s _understanding the system_. This includes:

- Undocumented dependencies between services,
- Tacit business rules buried in old tickets,
- Tribal knowledge about why certain hacks exist.

AI can’t fully solve this because systems are too fragmented for LLMs to ingest ([[AI struggles with unreproducible context]]). That’s why long-lived teams outperform constantly reshuffled ones: they amortize the "figuring out" cost over time ([[Stable teams compound institutional knowledge]]).
